# Build-up Questions (One-Line Answers)

**1. Why are registers faster than RAM?**  
Registers are inside the CPU, so they can be accessed much faster than RAM.

**2. What is the difference between the ALU and the Control Unit?**  
ALU performs calculations; the Control Unit tells every component what to do.

**3. What happens during the Fetch–Decode–Execute cycle?**  
The CPU fetches an instruction, decodes it, executes it, and repeats the process.

**4. What is the role of the Program Counter (PC)?**  
It stores the memory address of the next instruction to execute.

**5. What is the purpose of the Flag Register?**  
It stores the status of the last ALU operation (Zero, Carry, Sign, Overflow, etc.).

**6. Does a higher clock speed always mean better performance? Why?**  
No; performance also depends on IPC, cache, cores, and CPU architecture.

**7. Why can't the CPU execute instructions directly from a hard disk?**  
Because hard disks are too slow, instructions must first be loaded into RAM.

**8. How do the Control Unit, ALU, and Registers work together?**  
Registers hold data, the Control Unit controls the process, and the ALU performs the operation.
